Quick Answers About CIDA Registration

  • What CIDA means: The Construction Industry Development Authority is Sri Lanka's industry body for contractor registration and construction-sector standards.
  • Why it matters: It helps clients identify contractors with recognized industry standing and a more accountable delivery framework.
  • How Gaura uses it: We align our work with documentation, planning, and site-management discipline expected from a registered contractor.
  • Best use case: Home builds, commercial work, renovation, fitout, and projects that need a reliable project partner.

Why Clients Search for Construction Industry Development Authority

Many people search this term when they want a contractor they can verify before signing a proposal. That is a good instinct. Construction work is expensive, time-sensitive, and heavily dependent on coordination, so registration and recognition matter more than a low headline price.

What To Verify Before You Hire Any Contractor

If you are comparing options, do not stop at the registration claim. Ask for the items below and compare them side by side so the decision is based on facts, not sales language.

Registration Details

Confirm the contractor's registration status and whether the scope matches your project type.

BOQ and Scope

Check if pricing is tied to quantities, materials, and measurable deliverables.

Project References

Ask for recent completed work with photos, site visits, or client references where possible.

Approvals and Delivery

Review how the contractor handles approvals, site supervision, variations, and handover.
