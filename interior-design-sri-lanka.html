What interior design and fitout services include

Interior design is not only about visual styling. A complete interior scope may include planning how a room is used, selecting materials that suit maintenance and climate, coordinating lighting, storage, ceilings, joinery, wall finishes, furniture layouts, partitions, and fitout execution. When these parts are planned together, the result is cleaner, more efficient, and easier to build.

Home Interiors

Living spaces, bedrooms, kitchens, wardrobes, ceilings, and finish selections that support daily comfort.

Office and Commercial Fitouts

Workspaces, reception zones, display areas, and interior layouts designed for flow, brand presence, and usability.

Custom Interior Upgrades

Feature walls, lighting concepts, built-in storage, partitions, flooring, and tailored fitout solutions.

How we approach interior projects

  • Understand the lifestyle, business, or usage goals behind the space.
  • Plan layout, furniture flow, storage, and lighting direction first.
  • Choose materials and finishes that match both budget and maintenance needs.
  • Coordinate interior work with MEP and renovation needs where required.
  • Execute fitout work with finish consistency and practical sequencing.

Why interior planning matters

  • Better use of available floor area.
  • Cleaner visual balance and improved daily comfort.
  • Better lighting, storage, and circulation decisions.
  • Lower chance of finish clashes and late rework.
  • Stronger value for homes, offices, and commercial spaces.

Frequently asked questions

  • Do you offer interior design for homes in Sri Lanka? Yes. We support residential interiors including living areas, bedrooms, kitchens, wardrobes, and finish upgrades.
  • Can you handle office fitouts too? Yes. Gaura Construction supports office and commercial fitout work with layout and finish coordination.
  • Is fitout different from interior design? Interior design covers the planning and visual-functional direction, while fitout is the physical execution of that interior scope.
  • Can interior design be combined with renovation? Yes. Many interior projects benefit from combining renovation, MEP updates, and finishing work in one coordinated plan.
