Understanding Your Construction Contract
What Every Client Should Know

The clauses that actually protect you, explained without the legal jargon.

  • Gaura Editorial Team
  • Jul 22 2026
  • Comments 0

Most disputes between clients and contractors in Sri Lanka don't come from bad workmanship alone - they come from vague contracts that never clearly defined scope, payment terms, or what happens when something changes mid-project. Reading your contract properly before signing is the single best protection you have.

Scope of Work: Be Specific, Not General

A contract that simply says "construction of a two-storey house" leaves too much room for disagreement later. Look for a detailed scope referencing approved drawings, specifications, and a Bill of Quantities (BOQ) that lists exact materials and finishes included.

Payment Schedule Tied to Milestones

Payments should be linked to verifiable physical progress - foundation, structure, roofing, finishing - rather than calendar dates. This protects both sides: you don't pay for work that hasn't happened, and the contractor isn't left financing your project out of pocket.

Variations and Change Orders

Almost every project has some changes along the way. A solid contract specifies exactly how variations are priced, documented, and approved in writing before the extra work begins - avoiding disputes over surprise charges at the end.

A good contract isn't about distrust between client and contractor - it's what keeps both sides honest when memory alone isn't enough. - Gaura Construction

Timeline and Delay Clauses

Check what happens if the project runs late - whether there are penalty clauses, and whether delays caused by the client (late decisions, late payments) are treated differently from delays caused by the contractor.

Warranty and Defect Liability Period

A proper contract specifies a defect liability period after handover - typically 12 months - during which the contractor is obligated to fix workmanship issues at no extra cost.

Why We Put Everything in Writing

As the best construction company in Sri Lanka for transparent contracting, every Gaura Construction agreement includes a detailed BOQ, milestone-based payments, and a clear warranty period - so nothing is left to a verbal promise. Conclusion

A clear, detailed construction contract protects you just as much as it protects your contractor - it's worth the extra hour of reading before you sign. Contact us for a sample contract structure and BOQ format used on our projects.
