10 Common Construction Mistakes
to Avoid When Building in Sri Lanka

The mistakes we see most often - and simple planning steps that prevent every one of them.

  • Gaura Editorial Team
  • Jul 22 2026
  • Comments 0

After years of building across Sri Lanka, the same handful of mistakes keep showing up on projects that started with someone other than an established contractor. Most are avoidable with the right planning upfront.

1. Skipping the Soil Test and Land Survey

Building on unverified soil conditions is one of the most expensive mistakes possible. See our full guide on why a soil test and land survey matter before construction starts.

2. Choosing the Lowest Quote Without Checking the BOQ

A low quote with a vague scope almost always ends up costing more once missing items surface as "extras" mid-project. Always compare quotes against a detailed Bill of Quantities.

3. Treating MEP as an Afterthought

Electrical, plumbing, and mechanical planning done late leads to broken tiles, exposed conduits, and inadequate power distribution. Our MEP services guide explains why this needs to be planned from day one.

4. No Written Contract or Payment Schedule

Verbal agreements lead to disputes about scope and payment. See our construction contract guide for what should always be in writing.

Every one of these mistakes is cheap to prevent and expensive to fix. The difference is almost always planning, not luck. - Gaura Construction

5. Underestimating the Budget and Running Out Mid-Build

Projects that stall halfway due to funding shortfalls often cost more overall than properly budgeted ones. Use our construction cost calculator to set a realistic budget before you start.

6. Ignoring Local Authority Approvals

Building without proper approvals risks fines, demolition orders, or major delays in getting utility connections. Always confirm approvals are in hand before construction begins.

7. Not Planning for Sri Lanka's Monsoon Season

Poor drainage design and inadequate roof detailing lead to water damage that's expensive to reverse. Site drainage should be planned at foundation stage, not added later.

8. Hiring Separate, Uncoordinated Contractors

Splitting civil, MEP, and interior work across unrelated contractors creates coordination gaps and finger-pointing when something goes wrong - one of the reasons clients choose Gaura Construction as the best construction company in Sri Lanka for integrated project delivery.

9. No Defect Liability Period in Writing

Without a clear warranty period, post-handover issues can become a costly, drawn-out argument rather than a straightforward call-out.

10. Skipping Stage Inspections

Once walls are plastered, problems become invisible. Our construction quality checklist covers exactly what to inspect before each stage is covered up.

Conclusion

Nearly every costly construction mistake in Sri Lanka traces back to inadequate planning or working with an uncoordinated set of contractors. Contact us for a free consultation and site assessment before you break ground.
