What's Included in Each Finish Level?

Budget Finish

Basic structural works, plastered walls, cement screed floors, standard-grade fittings. Suitable for annexes, quarters or minimum-spec builds.

Standard Finish

Tiled floors, painted walls, standard sanitary fittings, basic electrical package. The entry point for a comfortable residential home.

Semi-Luxury Finish ★ Popular

Mid-grade imported tiles, feature walls, quality sanitary ware, modern kitchen cabinets, concealed wiring and full lighting plan. The most popular choice among Gaura clients.

Luxury Finish

Imported marble or large-format tiles, custom joinery, designer sanitary fittings, smart home pre-wiring, premium kitchen and wardrobe systems.

Premium / Signature Finish

Full architectural interior design, bespoke custom furniture, high-end imported finishes, full home automation, landscaping and external works included.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about construction costs per square foot in Sri Lanka

How much does it cost to build a 1,000 sqft house in Sri Lanka in 2026?

A 1,000 sqft house in Sri Lanka costs approximately:

  • Budget finish: LKR 6,000,000
  • Standard finish: LKR 7,000,000
  • Semi-Luxury: LKR 10,000,000
  • Luxury: LKR 15,000,000
  • Premium: LKR 30,000,000+

These figures are for a standard roofed (pitched roof) house. Box-type builds are 10–15% higher due to flat-roof waterproofing requirements.

How much does it cost to build a 2,000 sqft house in Sri Lanka in 2026?

A 2,000 sqft standard roofed house costs approximately:

  • Budget finish: LKR 12,000,000
  • Standard finish: LKR 14,000,000
  • Semi-Luxury: LKR 20,000,000
  • Luxury: LKR 30,000,000
  • Premium: LKR 60,000,000+

Is a box-type house more expensive to build than a roofed house in Sri Lanka?

Yes. Box-type (flat roof) houses typically cost 10–15% more per sqft than conventional pitched-roof houses in Sri Lanka. This is due to:

  • More complex waterproofing membrane systems required
  • Heavier slab loads compared to lightweight roof sheets
  • Higher concrete usage in the roof slab
  • Parapet walls and drainage detailing

For example, a 2,000 sqft standard-finish roofed house costs LKR 14,000,000 vs. LKR 16,000,000 for the same box-type house.

What factors affect construction cost per sqft in Sri Lanka?

The main factors that drive construction cost per square foot in Sri Lanka include:

  • House type — roofed, box-type, villa, mansion
  • Finish level — budget vs. premium materials can vary 5x
  • Location — Colombo metro costs more than outstation
  • Foundation — rock, soft soil, or pile foundations differ significantly
  • Design complexity — cantilevers, curves, multiple levels
  • MEP systems — full AC, solar, smart home features
  • Material prices — cement, steel and tile prices fluctuate

How can I reduce construction cost per sqft in Sri Lanka?

Proven ways to reduce your construction cost per sqft in Sri Lanka:

  • Choose a roofed design over box-type to save 10–15%
  • Minimise structural complexity — simple rectangular footprints cost less
  • Finalise your design before construction to avoid variation orders
  • Use locally-available tile sizes to reduce wastage
  • Combine MEP routes to reduce pipe and cable lengths
  • Work with a reputable contractor like Gaura Construction who procures in bulk

Does construction cost in Sri Lanka include land cost?

No. The construction cost per square foot figures on this page are for the building construction only and do not include:

  • Land purchase price
  • Legal and survey fees
  • Architectural and structural design fees (typically 2–5% of build cost)
  • External works such as boundary walls, gates, and landscaping (unless specified)
  • Connection fees for water, electricity and drainage
