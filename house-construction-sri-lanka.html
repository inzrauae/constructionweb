7. Common Mistakes to Avoid When Building a House

  • Skipping proper foundation assessment: Leads to settling, cracking, and costly structural repairs years later.
  • Poor MEP planning: Retrofitting electrical and plumbing causes expensive rework and disruption.
  • Late design changes: Mid-construction changes inflate costs and extend timelines significantly.
  • Underestimating finishing costs: Finishing often represents 30–40% of total project cost and must be budgeted upfront.
  • Using multiple uncoordinated subcontractors: Creates accountability gaps, quality inconsistency, and schedule overruns.
